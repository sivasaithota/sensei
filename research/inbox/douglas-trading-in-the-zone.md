# Trading in the Zone — Mark Douglas (Prentice Hall, 2000)
# Compiled from the book's widely published core framework.

A psychology book — its content is discipline, not signals. Documented core:

**The five fundamental truths:**
1. Anything can happen.
2. You don't need to know what will happen next in order to make money.
3. There is a random distribution between wins and losses for any given set
   of variables that define an edge.
4. An edge is nothing more than an indication of a higher probability of one
   thing happening over another.
5. Every moment in the market is unique.

**Operational implications Douglas draws:**
- Think in probabilities: a single trade's outcome is meaningless; only the
  distribution over many trades matters. Never judge a method on one trade.
- Predefine the risk of every trade before entry; accept that risk completely
  so no outcome causes hesitation or revenge.
- Consistency comes from executing the edge every time it appears — skipping
  valid signals out of fear, or taking invalid ones out of greed, destroys the
  measured edge.
- Do not carry the last trade into the next: errors are pattern-level
  (repeated deviations from plan), not outcome-level (a losing trade taken
  correctly is a good trade).
- The market owes you nothing; expectation of a specific outcome is the root
  of emotional interference.
