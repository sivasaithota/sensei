## The 3M framework
Gujral's stated foundation: Method, Money management, Mind-set. No method works
without fixed risk per trade and psychological discipline. He emphasizes: always
have a plan before entry; avoid overtrading; capture large moves rather than
booking many small profits; reduce size in volatile periods.

## Technical pillars (as documented)
1. **The 20 EMA is the trend line.** Gujral's central tool on every timeframe.
   Price above a rising 20 EMA = uptrend; the EMA acts as dynamic support.
   His classic entry: in an established uptrend, buy the pullback that touches
   or approaches the 20 EMA and then resumes upward — "buy the dip to the
   moving average in a trending market, never counter-trend."
2. **Trade only with the trend of the higher timeframe.** An intraday long needs
   the daily chart trending up; a daily-timeframe long is stronger when the
   weekly trend agrees. Shorter MA above longer MA confirms trend alignment.
3. **Exceptional candles.** Unusually large range candles with strong volume in
   the trend direction signal institutional participation; entries on/after
   such candles in trend direction have follow-through.
4. **Pivots and the central pivot range (CPR).** Prior-session pivot levels
   define the day's battlefield; trading above the pivot favors longs.
   A narrow CPR signals a likely trending day.
5. **Morning range.** The first 30-60 minutes' high/low; breakouts from it,
   in the direction of the larger trend, are his bread-and-butter intraday entry.
6. **Sector first, then stock.** Trade the strongest stocks in the strongest
   sectors; a stock signal against its sector is suspect.
7. **Gaps.** Gaps in trend direction that hold above the prior day's range tend
   to run; fading gaps against the bigger trend is low-probability.

## Money management (as documented)
Fixed small risk per trade (he cites ~1-2% max), stops always in the market,
position sized from the stop distance, and exits that trail winners rather than
capping them early.

## Notes for the examiner
- Items 4, 5 (pivot ranges, morning range) are intraday-session constructs and
  cannot be tested on daily bars — extract only if a daily-bar analogue is
  faithful, otherwise mark untestable.
- Item 6 requires sector index data not in the grammar — untestable for now.
- Items 1, 2, 3, 7 have daily-bar analogues: 20-DMA pullback-resume in an
  uptrend with MA alignment; wide-range up-candles on volume; gap-and-hold
  in trend direction.
