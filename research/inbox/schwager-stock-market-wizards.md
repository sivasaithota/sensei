# Stock Market Wizards — Jack D. Schwager (HarperBusiness, 2001)
# Compiled from the book's published interviews and Schwager's own documented
# synthesis of what the interviewed traders share.

Interviews with top-performing US stock traders (Minervini, Cook, Ryan, Bender,
Cohen, Shapiro, and others). Schwager's documented cross-trader conclusions:

1. **Risk control is the universal trait.** Every wizard rigorously limits loss
   per trade; methods differ wildly, risk discipline does not. Cut losses
   quickly and without debate; the stop is decided before entry.
2. **Trade a method that fits your personality** — copied systems fail when the
   trader can't psychologically execute them.
3. **An edge must be specific and repeatable**: each wizard can state exactly
   what conditions constitute their trade. Vague conviction is not an edge.
4. **Position size varies with conviction and conditions** — smaller in
   drawdowns and unfamiliar regimes, pressing only when the edge is clearest.
   Several wizards cut size in half after losing streaks.
5. **Patience over action**: most wizards trade less than observers expect;
   they wait for their exact setup and do nothing otherwise (Cook: "the market
   pays you to wait").
6. **Losses are tuition**: every wizard keeps records and reviews mistakes
   systematically; repeated review of losing trades is where improvement lives.
7. Documented common tactical thread (Ryan, Minervini): buy strength — stocks
   making new highs out of sound bases in strong markets — not weakness;
   average up, never down.
