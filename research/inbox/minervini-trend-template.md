# Mark Minervini — Trend Template (from "Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard", 2013)

Minervini's Trend Template defines the conditions a stock must meet before he
will consider buying. The stock must be in a confirmed Stage 2 uptrend:

1. The current price is above both the 150-day and the 200-day moving average.
2. The 150-day moving average is above the 200-day moving average.
3. The 200-day moving average is trending up (at least 1 month, preferably 4-5 months).
4. The 50-day moving average is above both the 150-day and the 200-day moving averages.
5. The current price is trading above the 50-day moving average.
6. The current price is at least 30 percent above its 52-week low.
7. The current price is within at least 25 percent of its 52-week high (the
   closer to a new high the better).
8. Relative strength: the stock shows superior price performance versus the
   market over the prior 6 months.

Entries are made on breakouts from proper bases (volatility contraction) within
this template, with volume expanding on the breakout day. Stops are cut quickly
— Minervini risks a few percent at most, never letting a loss exceed roughly
7-8 percent. He sells into strength with profits typically taken around 2-3x
his risk.

# Larry Connors — RSI(2) pullback (from "Short Term Trading Strategies That Work", 2008)

Buy pullbacks within uptrends: when a stock is above its 200-day moving average
and the 2-period RSI closes below 5, buy; exit when price closes above its
5-day moving average, typically within days. No stop is used in the book's
tests, but any live implementation should cap the loss; holding periods are
short, usually 2-7 days.
