# How to Make Money Trading with Candlestick Charts — Balkrishna Sadekar (Vision Books)
# Compiled from the publisher's description, published reviews, and the classical
# candlestick pattern definitions (Nison lineage) the book teaches for Indian markets.

Core stance: candlestick patterns are only meaningful in CONTEXT — a reversal
pattern needs a prior trend to reverse, and confirmation from the next candle
and volume. Patterns against the prevailing larger trend are low-probability.

Bullish patterns taught (long-side, as used for Indian equities):
1. **Bullish engulfing** after a decline: a green body that completely engulfs
   the prior red body; stronger with above-average volume and when it forms at
   or near a support/moving-average zone in a larger uptrend.
2. **Hammer** after a decline: small body near the candle's top with a lower
   shadow at least twice the body; buy on the follow-through, not the hammer
   itself — confirmation is the next candle closing above the hammer's high.
3. **Piercing pattern / morning star** after a decline: multi-candle reversal;
   the essence is a strong close reclaiming most of the prior red candle.
4. **Strong close in the top of the range** on expanding volume marks
   institutional demand; weak closes at lows mark distribution.
5. **Inside-day (harami) resolution**: a quiet inside candle after a big move,
   resolved by a close beyond the inside candle's range in the trend direction.

Risk framing: every pattern trade has its stop at the pattern's extreme (hammer
low, engulfing low); if the pattern's low breaks, the signal is dead. Targets
modest — patterns are entries, trend does the earning.
