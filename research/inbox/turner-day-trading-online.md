# A Beginner's Guide to Day Trading Online — Toni Turner (Adams Media, 2nd ed. 2007)
# Compiled from the publisher's description and widely published summaries.

A basics book; most content is intraday craft and platform mechanics, but its
swing-applicable core principles as documented:

1. **Trade with the trend of the next-higher timeframe.** Day trades follow the
   daily trend; swing trades follow the weekly. Never fight the bigger tide.
2. **Volume confirms price.** Breakouts and reversals count only when volume
   expands versus its recent average; price moves on quiet volume revert.
3. **Support/resistance from prior highs and lows**: buy strength that clears a
   recent consolidation high; the prior consolidation acts as the risk floor.
4. **Minimum 2:1 reward-to-risk** before entering any trade; if the stop
   distance doesn't allow it, skip the trade.
5. **Avoid the chop**: most losses come from trading when there is no trend —
   flat moving averages, narrow ranges. Stand aside in dull, directionless tape.
6. Risk management: never risk more than a small fixed percentage per trade;
   respect stops without exception; scale into competence with small size.
